# Compliance Matrix — AgenticMedicalImagingHelper

> This document maps the project's existing controls to the obligations of the **EU AI Act** (Regulation 2024/1689) and the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0**. It is a working artefact, not a conformity assessment — gaps are listed honestly so a downstream auditor can see what remains.
>
> The project is a CLI **research/educational tool**, not a CE-marked medical device. Section 1 clarifies why; the matrices that follow assume any future productisation would require the controls listed under "Gap".

---

## 1. Regulatory positioning

| Question                                                           | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Is this a medical device under EU MDR (2017/745) / FDA SaMD?       | **No.** The README and every output enforce an "educational and informational purposes only" disclaimer. Outputs are not approved for clinical decision-making.                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Is this a **high-risk** AI system under EU AI Act Annex III?       | **Conditionally.** Annex III §5(a) (medical-device safety components) and §5(c) (essential public services) become applicable _if_ the system is ever marketed for clinical use. Today: out of scope. As a research tool intended for healthcare workflows it sits closer to a **limited-risk** Article 50 transparency obligation. |
| Is the system **placed on the EU market** or **put into service**? | **No** — distributed as open source for personal / research use. Article 2 obligations attach only on commercial deployment.                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Are the foundation-model provider's obligations inherited?         | **Partially.** The project uses Google `gemini-2.5-pro`. The provider carries the GPAI obligations of Article 51 ff.; the project carries the _deployer_ obligations of Article 26 only if/when deployed commercially.                                                                                                              |

**Practical implication.** Today the project owes (a) honesty about its educational scope, (b) the transparency obligations of Article 13, and (c) defensible engineering hygiene. The matrices below show how the existing code already meets those, and what extra would be needed for an Annex III deployment.
